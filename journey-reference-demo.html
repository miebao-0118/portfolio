How I found my way in

Origins

Kerch, Ukraine. 1994.

Born between two seas, one landline, and zero patience for being bored.

In 2001, a 56K dialup screamed its way into our house and into my life. Mom hated it. I thought it felt magical.

An entire universe on the other end of a cable, and me, impatient to break into it.

Going places

Rapid City, SD. 2009.

As a teen: soldering robots, DC++ swaps, IRC friends I would never meet, pirated game discs, and a habit of poking at systems until they gave something back.

Then came the move, the first job, American high school strangeness, weekends in the snow, and a sharper sense that the internet was not just a place to hide in.

It was where identity, work, and access were all starting to blur together.

The turning point

Porto, PT. 2020.

Years later, after product, sales, support, and too many messy client conversations, one thing became obvious: the best work happens when you stop performing and start solving.

I moved to Porto, went remote-first, and started building the kind of client relationships I always wished existed. Clear thinking, product depth, no theater.

Still the same curious kid with the modem, just on the other side of the connection now.

Single sticky collage, not step-swapped
